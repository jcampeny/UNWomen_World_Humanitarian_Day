Syria refugee crisis

UN Women’s largest cash-forwork programme for women in Jordan’s Za’atari refugee camp increased the average income of full-time workers, improving diets, household hygiene and healthcare.

Ebola epidemic, West Africa

Informational radio programmes, psychosocial support and UN Womenfacilitated trainings helped women adopt new strategies to respond to, cope with and curb the spread of Ebola.

Earthquake, Nepal

When an earthquake rocked Nepal in 2015, UN Women partnered with national women’s groups and civil society to develop an early recovery strategy through a “Common Charter of Women’s Demands”.

Cyclone, Fiji

Following the devastating cyclone in Fiji in 2016, UN Women has been working with local partners to promote economic opportunities for women through market spaces.

When crises strike, gender inequalities are often exacerbated

These include: increased levels of genderbased violence, exclusion from life-saving services and decision-making processes due to discriminatory social norms, such as food hierarchies, and limited mobility to get help due to physical insecurity.
